# DM Speaking Notes (Read Aloud for Recording Test)

Read these naturally, pausing between sections. You're narrating as a DM.

---

## ACT 1 — The Dust Settles

Alright, so... the last echoes of the fight fade. There's this ringing silence now. The air smells like ozone and iron — blood, basically. You can feel the weight of what just happened.

You look over at Allryn. They're barely breathing. Their skin is ice cold, and there's this faint magical shimmer flickering around them — like their life force is unstable. They are in critical condition. Every few seconds, the shimmer pulses weaker.

And then... Melora. You check on Melora. There's nothing. No pulse. No breath. She's gone. There's no death saving throws here — Melora is dead. Just stillness.

Now here's the thing — Allryn is getting worse. Whatever hit them, conventional healing isn't going to cut it. You can stabilize them temporarily, but they need a real specialist. Someone with serious power. And you don't have a lot of time.

---

## ACT 2 — The Grief and the Burden

Torvin, your dwarf follower, just drops to his knees. He can't process it. Meanwhile Sera — she goes quiet. Like scary quiet. She starts packing gear mechanically, just going into survival mode.

So here's the choice you need to make. Do you take Melora's body with you? Or do you leave her here to move faster and save Allryn? Because carrying both of them through the Ashfell Pass is going to slow you down considerably.

Oh — and you find it. The Starweave Amulet. The thing you came here for. It's sitting right there in the dust. But picking it up... it feels heavy. Wrong. Like the cost was too high.

## [OFF-SCRIPT — Not in the plan]

Actually, before you leave, something unexpected happens. A figure steps out from behind the collapsed pillar. It's a tiefling woman, hooded, hands raised. She says her name is Vex Nighthollow, and she's been watching the fight. She says she's a member of the Hollow Court — a faction you've never heard of. She offers to carry Melora's body for you, but she wants the amulet in return. She says "the dead deserve better than dusty ruins."

This is not a fight — she's genuinely offering help. But trusting a stranger from an unknown faction with your dead friend's body AND the artifact you nearly died for? That's your call.

---

## ACT 3 — The Desperate Journey

Alright, skill challenge time. You need to get through the Ashfell Pass to reach Thornwall before Allryn's soul slips away.

First — someone needs to tend to Allryn while you travel. Medicine or Arcana check. You're literally keeping their soul tethered to their body. The magical instability keeps spiking.

Next — Athletics or Survival. The terrain through Ashfell Pass is brutal. Loose scree, narrow ledges, and you're carrying wounded. One wrong step and you're all going down.

Then — you reach the gates of Thornwall. It's the middle of the night. The gates are sealed. You need Persuasion or Intimidation to convince the gate captain to let a bloody, desperate group of adventurers into the city and direct you to Mother Ashwick's healing sanctum.

Oh, and on the road — a group of scavengers spots you. Three of them, picking through old battlefield remains. They see Allryn's condition and figure you're easy marks. It's a quick fight but it feels like an insult on top of everything.

---

## [OFF-SCRIPT — Not in the plan]

During the skill challenge, Torvin suddenly confesses something. He says he knew about the trap that killed Melora. He'd found a warning inscription earlier in the ruins but couldn't read it in time. He's been carrying this guilt the entire time. This is a huge character moment — Torvin revealing this secret changes how the party sees him.

---

## ACT 4 — The Sanctuary

You finally reach Mother Ashwick's sanctum. She's ancient, powerful, and not happy about being woken up. She examines Allryn and says she can save them — but there's a price.

She wants Melora's shield. The enchanted shield Melora carried. She says the enchantment on it contains residual life energy that she can channel into the healing ritual. Without it, she doesn't have enough power.

So that's another gut punch — giving up one of the last pieces of Melora to save Allryn.

And then — while Allryn is under, hanging between life and death, they have a vision. They see Melora in this soft golden light. She looks peaceful. She says one thing: "The Starweave remembers what the living forget. Trust the thread, not the weaver." And then the vision fades.

Last image of the session: the party sitting in a dim hallway outside the surgery room. Exhausted. Bloodied. Sera is cleaning her blade. Torvin is staring at the wall. And the door closes as Mother Ashwick begins the ritual. We end there.
